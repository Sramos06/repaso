Hypothesis testing

State the null and alternative. Choose the level of significance. Compute the test statistic and compare against the critical region.

review the z vs t choice ✎

Reject the null when the statistic falls in the rejection region. Otherwise, fail to reject. Interpret in context of the problem, never in isolation.

For proportions use the normal approximation when np and nq are both at least five.

V2Ink stroke

Measures of dispersion

Range, variance, standard deviation. The standard deviation speaks the same units as the data, which is why it is the one you quote.

Chebyshev holds for any distribution; the empirical rule needs the bell.

memorize 68 95 99.7

Coefficient of variation compares spread across different scales.

V3Washi
A strip of sage tape you slide along the paper's edge, textured like the tape on the desk cards.
Sampling distributions

The mean of the sampling distribution equals the population mean. The standard error shrinks with the square root of n.

Central limit theorem: for large n, the sampling distribution of the mean is approximately normal regardless of the population.

n at least 30, usually ✎

Finite population correction applies when sampling without replacement from a small population.

V4Ribbon
The terracotta bookmark ribbon of a hardbound notebook, hanging down the page edge.
Correlation and regression

Pearson's r measures linear association between minus one and one. Correlation is not causation, ever, on the exam and in life.

The least squares line minimizes the sum of squared residuals.

r squared = explained share

Extrapolating beyond the observed range of x is how predictions go wrong.

V5Stitch
A sewn seam: dotted thread for the track, a smooth ink knot for the thumb. Bookbinding energy.
Probability rules

Addition rule for unions, multiplication rule for intersections, complements for at-least-one problems.

Conditional probability rescales the sample space to what is known.

independence: P(A|B) = P(A)

Bayes' theorem reverses the conditioning, which is where intuition usually fails.
