Repaso · Round 2 · The v1.5 App Shell · July 10, 2026

Same warm paper.
Three ways to organize the desk.

All three keep your chosen Repaso theme and show the same new features — search, subjects, exam countdowns, pins, rename/delete (the ⋯ menu), settings with logout & export — but arrange the app around three different philosophies. Every one is clickable with real flows: open a reviewer, try the notes, tap the ⋯ menus, poke the settings. No hamburgers were harmed.

mix & match welcome — e.g. "A's desk + B's notes tab + C's split viewer"
DIRECTION ADesk & Drawers

Your current app, grown up. One screen stays king: search slides into the header, subject chips + exam countdown strip live above the cards, every card gets a pin + ⋯ menu, and settings tuck behind your avatar. Notes keep the slide-out drawer but gain a Write/Preview toggle. Philosophy: change nothing you already like, bolt on what's missing.

Open A →
DIRECTION BBinder

A true phone app. Floating bottom tabs — Desk / Notes / Exams / Settings — like the apps you already live in. Notes become a first-class place: browse ALL your notes in one tab, and inside a reviewer you flip between full-width "Reviewer" and "Notes" tabs (your idea!) instead of a cramped drawer. Exams get a whole screen with linked reviewers. Philosophy: Repaso is a mobile app that happens to run on desktop.

Open B →
DIRECTION CCommand Desk

A study workstation. Sidebar with your subjects as a colored tree + pinned + next-exam widget; a big Ctrl-K search bar that hunts inside reviewer content; and the killer feature: the viewer opens in split view — reviewer left, notes right, both visible while you study (with a Focus toggle). Sidebar collapses to icons on phone. Philosophy: built for long desktop study sessions.

Open C →

How to judge: open each full-screen → use the search → tap a reviewer → try the notes (this is the big differentiator: drawer vs. tab vs. split) → find logout/export in each → squeeze the window narrow for the phone feel.
